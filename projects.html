  • A3T (Drone Team Twente)

    Once again, I got bored and wanted to work on something hands-on, this time I was getting into robotics and drones.   • Project X

    As a naive freshman in college, I was super bored with classes and was looking for something to work on hands-on. I was travelling to college everyday on a scooter, and wanted to upgrade to a motorcycle. I wanted an electric one, but couldn't find one. I decided to make my own. I drew up a few skecthes and specifications, and talked to a couple of friends about the project. They really liked it, and I realised other people might want one too, so I put together a team and started working on it. I made the mistake of wanting to build all major components ourselves, but realised we were in way over our heads. I'm talking ground up power electronics desin like a 30KW AC motor controller, a 6kWh Li-ion Battery management board etc.

    However, we managed to get support from the University as well as wiin some funds through a national competition. In the meanwhile the year was ending and an opportunity for me to study at a University abroad came up that I couldn't pass up. So unfortunately I had to leave the project, but thankfully the team was still there! The team ended up building a nice prototype and got some recognition for it. They eventually established an EV club at the University.
